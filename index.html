My Work Experience At Credersi

04.07.22 - 08.07.22

This past week, I have taken part in many things, that have in turn given me a great insight into the world of work, here I will retell my experiences:

Monday

On the first day i worked with Dr. D. First, i researched what he was working on- an enzyme called glucose-6-phosphate-dehydrogenase (or G6PD), in doing this i found it had a pivotal role in the first step of the pentose phosphate pathway (PPP) which is a series of chemical reactions that convert glucose to another sugar; ribose-5-phosphate. The enzyme also helps protect red blood cells from harmful substances in the blood that could damage them. Without enough of this enzyme, red blood cells can break apart and symptoms of hemolytic anemia will be observed.

Tuesday

Second day, and here I packaged several practical boxes for two different experiments, after this i reasearched a 'one-atom thick' future in the uses of 'wonder' material graphene. I found that in medicine; they are using temporary graphene tattoos that continuously monitor arterial blood pressure. 'White coat syndrome' and nervousness at the doctors elevate heart rate when its measured therefore possibly resulting in an inaccurate reading, so this self adhesive 'tattoo' can be placed on the radial and ulner arteries using the graphene as 'ink'. Graphene is an electrical conductor so a tiny current is run throught that allows bioimpendance measures.
